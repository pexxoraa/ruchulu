Our Story

Every jar starts in someone's kitchen.

Home / Our Story

Ruchulu began with one recipe: a grandmother's avakaya, made every mango season in Vijayawada and shared with anyone who dropped by. Neighbours started asking for jars of their own. Then their children, living in Hyderabad and Bengaluru and further afield, started asking too.

What we built is not a factory. It's a small collective of home cooks across Andhra Pradesh and Telangana, each one responsible for the recipes they know best — a family's pickle, a grandmother's podi, a household's festival snack. We package what they make, exactly as they make it, and get it to your door before it loses that just-made freshness.

"We don't sell pickle. We send home a little bit of Andhra."

No preservatives, no shortcuts, no centralised kitchen smoothing out the differences that make each recipe someone's own. If a jar tastes slightly different from batch to batch, that's not inconsistency — that's the cook, and the season's mangoes, doing what they always do.
